What makes this an appealing project?

  • The Diep River is a backbone of this corridor. By following a river, there is potential to use an existing ecological corridor.
  • The Diep River has poor water quality and is often a liability in social and environmental terms. By working on restoring the vegetation around the river, the actual river receives attention. We recommend linking this project with additional river cleanup and restoration efforts.
  • The selected area is currently not close to high-quality nature, based on the Bionet network. By working on gardens in this area, there is an opportunity to reduce the distance to nature for people living here.

Ward profiles

This corridor traverses 2 very distinct wards: ward 62 and ward 72. We recommend that bridging the social divides between these wards is a fundamental part of corridor building. This can also include using resources from the wealthier parts to support activities in the less affluent parts. Please find the relevant ward profiles below.
